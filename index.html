THE ILLUSION OF THE OBVIOUS

A New Physics of Consciousness and Death

After fourteen years of independent theoretical research, this framework proposes a comprehensive solution to some of physics' deepest mysteries: the quantum measurement problem, the nature of consciousness, the composition of dark energy and dark matter, and the persistence of identity after death.

Through eight-dimensional substrate architecture—four familiar spacetime dimensions plus four substrate dimensions—consciousness emerges as a fundamental force, photons are reconceptualized as fracture events, and death transforms from annihilation into dimensional transition.

The framework generates testable predictions while addressing humanity's most profound questions about awareness, meaning, and existence.

ABOUT THE FRAMEWORK

This work proposes an eight-dimensional substrate architecture that unifies quantum mechanics, consciousness, cosmology, and the nature of death into a coherent physical theory.

CORE PROPOSALS

Photons Reconceptualized

Photons are not particles carrying images but fracture events—electromagnetic oscillations in substrate dark energy crossing dimensional boundaries. Light carries only four numerical parameters (frequency, amplitude, phase, polarization). Visual experience emerges from consciousness rendering neural patterns, not from images in light.

Consciousness as Fundamental Force

Consciousness is proposed as the fifth fundamental force, generated at the substrate-void boundary and coupling to matter through dark energy fields. Coupling strength follows the formula G ∝ N·⟨g²⟩·C, where N is neuron count, g is coupling constant, and C is coherence factor.

Quantum Measurement Resolved

Wave function collapse occurs when consciousness coupling exceeds threshold Gc, actualizing quantum superposition to definite outcome through projection operators. This resolves the measurement problem without Many Worlds or hidden variables.

Dark Sector Explained

Dark energy and dark matter are not mysterious unknown particles but substrate dimensions. Dark energy (ρDE ≈ 10-29 g/cm³) results from projection geometry. Dark matter is dimensional mass coupling gravitationally without electromagnetic interaction.

Death as Transformation

Biological death ends neural consciousness coupling but doesn't eliminate consciousness itself. Pattern-identity—complete actualization history holographically encoded in dark information dimension—persists eternally, enabling continued consciousness coupling in substrate structural time.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Frank Di Leo is an independent researcher and retired eye surgeon. After decades in ophthalmology, he devoted fourteen years to developing this comprehensive theoretical framework addressing fundamental questions in physics, consciousness, and cosmology.

At age one, Di Leo survived a catastrophic accident in Manhattan when a 25-ton dump truck plowed through a crosswalk, destroying his pram and catapulting him half a block. Despite the violence of the impact, he emerged without a scratch—physically unharmed yet profoundly marked. From early childhood, he experienced what he describes as an inexplicable preoccupation with dimensions beyond the visible and the nature of death, as though the accident had granted him a brief glimpse into the substrate reality this framework proposes.

Working independently outside traditional academic institutions, Di Leo pursued what became a "mysterious compulsion" to understand the deep structure of reality. This manuscript represents the culmination of that sustained inquiry, proposing testable mechanisms for phenomena that have long resisted physical explanation—including the very consciousness transitions he may have briefly experienced as an infant.

The framework integrates insights from quantum mechanics, neuroscience, mathematics, cosmology, and philosophy of mind into a unified eight-dimensional substrate architecture. Di Leo's background in medicine, particularly in the precise observational demands of eye surgery, informed his approach to theoretical physics: careful attention to detail, systematic investigation, and respect for both empirical evidence and conceptual coherence.

If the framework's proposal is correct—that near-death experiences offer glimpses of substrate consciousness as neural coupling weakens—then Di Leo's lifelong theoretical quest may represent an attempt to understand, through physics and mathematics, what he once accessed directly but could not consciously remember.
